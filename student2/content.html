To enhance the resilience of marine habitats and species

In order to diversify the resilience of marine biota and conduct, carrying out adaptable measures is irreplaceable. Conservation also incorporates restoring and protecting the most essential ocean ecosystems, such as coral reefs, mangroves, and seagrass beds, which are a basic and effective habitat and ensure diversity. Besides, the strategy to create marine protected areas, to implement sustainable fisheries management practices, and to reduce wounding are all forces that ensure resilience when impacts by the climate change emerge. .


Promoting sustainable coastal development is vital for reducing vulnerability to sea-level rise and extreme weather events.



The necessity of careful coastal development which confines damage proneness to sea level rise and more intense weather events cannot be understated. This encumbers coast zone and land-use planning laws and regulations that deal with further habitat destruction and also decrease erosion hazards. Funding green infrastructure including the coastal natural shoreline buffers and their living shorelines counterparts will serve two things, they will provide better coastal protection while preserving the coastal ecosystems. .

Supporting research and innovation for climate-smart ocean solutions is critical for addressing the challenges posed by climate change


The research of and innovation for climate profficient ocean solutions is a top priority if dealing with the threats posed by the problem of climate change. This demands finding, and using ocean-related technologies, and practices, such as climate change mitigation and adaptation innovations. These samples include geoengineering, such as ocean fertilization which involves adding organic bits to the ocean to promote the growth of phytoplankton that create carbonate minerals that sequester carbon, and harnessing renewable energy resources like tidal power generation, wave-power generation and offshore wind energy. Moreover, government funding for scientific research and monitoring activities will be helpful in validating the datum on the amount of impact of climate change on marine environments and lateral assistance in making well-informed and futuristic decisions regarding marine management.
